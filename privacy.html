Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 2026

Data Controller: Evidence-Based Health • evidencebasedhealth.me • United States • info@evidencebasedhealth.me

This policy explains what personal data we collect, why we collect it, how long we keep it, with whom we share it, and what rights you have over it.

1. What we collect

When you subscribe or visit the site, we may collect:

  • Email address — required to send you the newsletter or prevention reminders
  • Phone number — only if you opt in to SMS updates
  • IP address — captured at subscription time for spam prevention and compliance records
  • User agent / browser info — captured at subscription time
  • Page you subscribed from — for internal analytics
  • Cookie identifiers — if you consent to analytics or marketing cookies (see Section 7)

2. Legal bases for processing (GDPR Article 6)

Activity Legal basis GDPR Article
Sending the newsletter you signed up for Consent — you explicitly subscribed Art. 6(1)(a)
Sending SMS updates (if opted in) Consent — SMS consent checkbox Art. 6(1)(a)
Sending prevention reminders Consent — you chose to save your profile Art. 6(1)(a)
Remarketing (hashed email to ad platforms) Consent — remarketing checkbox at sign-up Art. 6(1)(a)
Analytics and marketing cookies Consent — cookie banner Art. 6(1)(a)
Storing IP address and user agent Legitimate interest — fraud and spam prevention Art. 6(1)(f)

Where we rely on consent, you may withdraw it at any time without affecting the lawfulness of processing carried out before withdrawal.

3. Third parties we share data with

We use the following services to operate this site:

  • Amazon Web Services (AWS) — hosting (Amplify), email (SES), SMS (Pinpoint), and database (DynamoDB). Data processed in the us-east-1 (N. Virginia) region. AWS Privacy Notice
  • Meta (Facebook) — if you accept marketing cookies, the Meta Pixel fires on page views and on subscription. If you consented to remarketing, we may upload your hashed email to create custom audiences. Meta Privacy Policy
  • Google — if you accept marketing cookies, Google Ads tags fire to measure conversions and build remarketing audiences. If you consented to remarketing, we may upload your hashed email to Google Ads. Google Privacy Policy
  • Buffer — used to schedule social media content. No subscriber data is shared with Buffer.
  • Make (Integromat) — used for content automation. No subscriber PII is passed through Make.

We do not sell your personal information to third parties.

4. International data transfers

Evidence-Based Health uses AWS infrastructure located in the United States. If you are in the European Economic Area (EEA) or the United Kingdom, your data is transferred to and processed in the United States. We rely on the EU–U.S. Data Privacy Framework and, where applicable, Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) as our lawful transfer mechanism. AWS participates in the Data Privacy Framework; details are available at dataprivacyframework.gov.

5. Prevention Profiles

If you use the Prevention Roadmap tool and choose to save your profile, we collect the following additional information:

  • Year of birth — used to calculate approximate age for guideline-based suggestions. We do not collect your full date of birth.
  • Sex assigned at birth — used only to apply the correct screening guidelines where they differ by reproductive biology.
  • Organ / anatomy selections — checkboxes you select (e.g., “I have a colon”). Used to surface relevant screening topics.
  • Optional risk flags — e.g., family history of colorectal cancer. Used only to adjust age thresholds in guideline-based suggestions.
  • Email and optional phone number — used to send age-appropriate prevention reminders.
  • IP address and device information — collected for abuse prevention and logging.

What we do not collect: your name, full date of birth, address, insurance information, or any clinical records. Prevention Roadmap data is never used for diagnosis, treatment decisions, employment, or insurance purposes. It is used solely to send educational, age-appropriate screening reminders.

Reminders are sent no more than once per year per screening topic. To stop reminder emails, use the unsubscribe link in any reminder email or reply STOP to any SMS. To request deletion of your prevention profile, email info@evidencebasedhealth.me.

If you consented to remarketing, your email (hashed before transmission) may be used to create custom audiences on advertising platforms for the purpose of showing you relevant educational content. You can withdraw this consent at any time by contacting us.

6. Automated decision-making

We do not use automated decision-making or profiling to make decisions that produce legal or similarly significant effects on you. The Prevention Roadmap tool applies published clinical guidelines locally in your browser to suggest relevant topics; no automated decision with significant effect is made about any individual.

7. Cookies and your preferences

We use three categories of cookies and browser storage:

  • Essential — required for the site to function correctly. No consent needed.
  • Analytics — help us understand aggregate traffic patterns. Used only with your consent.
  • Marketing — advertising platform tags that enable conversion measurement and remarketing. Used only with your consent.

When you first visit the site, a cookie consent banner asks for your preference. Your choice is stored in your browser’s localStorage under the key cookieConsent. To change your preference, clear your browser’s site data for evidencebasedhealth.me and reload the page, or contact us and we will update your record.

8. Your rights

Depending on your location, you have the following rights:

  • Access — request a copy of the personal data we hold about you (GDPR Art. 15)
  • Rectification — ask us to correct inaccurate data (GDPR Art. 16)
  • Erasure — ask us to delete your data (“right to be forgotten”) (GDPR Art. 17)
  • Restriction — ask us to restrict processing of your data (GDPR Art. 18)
  • Portability — receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format (GDPR Art. 20)
  • Objection — object to processing based on legitimate interests (GDPR Art. 21)
  • Withdraw consent — withdraw any consent at any time without affecting prior lawful processing
  • Unsubscribe from email — use the unsubscribe link in any email, or contact us directly
  • Opt out of SMS — reply STOP to any SMS we send
  • Opt out of remarketing — contact us and we will remove your email from any audience lists
  • Lodge a complaint — if you are in the EEA or UK, you have the right to lodge a complaint with your local supervisory authority (e.g., the ICO in the United Kingdom at ico.org.uk, or your national data protection authority)
  • California / CPRA — California residents have additional rights including the right to know, delete, and opt out of the sale or sharing of personal information. We do not sell personal information.

To exercise any of these rights, email info@evidencebasedhealth.me. We will respond within 30 days (or within the statutory period if shorter under applicable law).

9. Data retention

10. Security

Subscriber and profile data is stored in AWS DynamoDB with encryption at rest (AES-256) and in transit (TLS). Access is restricted to authorized personnel via IAM roles with least-privilege permissions. We do not store payment information.

11. Children

This site is not directed at children under 13 (or under 16 in the EEA / UK where applicable). We do not knowingly collect personal data from anyone below these ages. If you believe we have done so, please contact us immediately.

12. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. Material changes will be noted by updating the “Last updated” date above and, where required by law, by notifying you directly. Continued use of the site or newsletter after a material change constitutes acknowledgement of the updated policy.

13. Contact

For any privacy-related questions or to exercise your rights, email info@evidencebasedhealth.me.
